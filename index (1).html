About

My research sits at the intersection of farm financial management and conservation agriculture. Working with Kansas Farm Management Association (KFMA) panel data and field-level precision agriculture records, I study how practices like no-till and cover cropping shape farm profitability, credit access, and resilience to weather and cash-flow shocks. I also contribute to applied policy work on the farm safety net, including analysis of commodity programs and crop insurance under recent legislative changes.

I'm advised by Dr. Jennifer Ifft in the Department of Agricultural Economics at Kansas State University. Before K-State, I earned my undergraduate degree at Zamorano University (Escuela Agrícola Panamericana) in Honduras.

Conference Papers

Farm Debt Dynamics Following Cash Flow Shocks
with Gaku and Jennifer Ifft · AAEA Annual Meeting 2026, Kansas City
Abstract

Using KFMA panel data (2013–2020) and a rolling z-score shock framework, this paper traces how weather and cash-flow shocks move through farm balance sheets. Accumulated heat stress significantly reduces current and total loan volume, with the largest credit contraction on high-leverage farms and distinct borrowing responses among young producers.

Extension & Outreach

Healthy Soil, Healthy Bottom Line is an extension program I lead under K-State Regenerative Agriculture, translating peer-reviewed findings on soil health economics into decision tools for producers. Through four in-person delivery events, the program has reached more than 220 Kansas producers, with pre/post evaluation benchmarked against the original KFMA study sample.

The program was developed for the 2026 AAEA Extension Competition and continues as an ongoing outreach effort.
